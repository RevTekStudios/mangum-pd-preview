MEET THE DEPARTMENT

The officers who serve Mangum

The Mangum Police Department is made up of dedicated officers, investigators, and reserve personnel who bring experience, professionalism, and a commitment to serving the community with pride.

COMMAND STAFF

Department Leadership

Leadership at Mangum Police Department is built on experience, training, and a commitment to professional service.

Chief of Police

Chief Lance Taylor

Chief Taylor retired from the Army after 20 years of service before beginning his law enforcement career in Granite, Oklahoma in 2011.

He later served with Elk City Police Department, where he rose to the rank of Sergeant, before becoming Chief of Police in Mangum in 2023.

Focus: CLEET certified instructor, firearms training

Assistant Chief

Major Josh Olson

Major Olson began his law enforcement career in 2016 with Elk City Police Department and joined Mangum Police Department in 2023.

He serves as the department investigator, is an ICAC investigator, and works with the Greer MDT team.

Focus: Investigations, ICAC, Greer MDT

FULL-TIME OFFICERS

Professional service on the street

Our full-time officers bring a range of experience, talent, and specialized strengths to the department.

Corporal

Aaron Lowry

Corporal Lowry was the first officer hired into the new Mangum Police Department in 2023. Mangum was his first department, and he has played an important role in helping build the agency.

He is currently a certified Field Training Officer and is pursuing CLEET instructor certification.

Focus: Complex warrants, financial crimes, field training

Officer

Chase Wood

Officer Wood began his law enforcement career in Elk City in 2018 and joined Mangum Police Department in 2025 as a veteran officer.

He brings experience and a strong investigative mindset to the department’s patrol and enforcement efforts.

Focus: Drug crimes, financial crimes

Officer

Syncere Loftis

Officer Loftis is the department’s newest officer and is graduating CLEET this month.

He has strong people skills, is an excellent student, and has already shown the kind of professionalism and promise the department believes will lead to a very successful career.

Focus: Public interaction, professionalism, growth potential
