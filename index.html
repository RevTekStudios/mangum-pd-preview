DEPARTMENT LEADERSHIP

Leadership rooted in service, integrity, and community trust

Under the leadership of Chief Lance Taylor, the Mangum Police Department is committed to protecting life, property, and the constitutional rights of all while serving the community with professionalism, compassion, and respect.

Our department believes public safety is strengthened through strong relationships, ethical leadership, and a shared commitment to justice. By working alongside citizens, schools, and community partners, we strive to maintain a safe and secure environment for everyone in Mangum.

DEPARTMENT VALUES

The principles that guide our service

These core values shape the way the Mangum Police Department serves the public, works with the community, and upholds trust.

Integrity

We do what is right, treat all people with respect, and take pride in honest public service.

Communication

We believe citizens should have access to their department and a voice in the quality of life of the community.

Professionalism

We hold ourselves accountable and commit to service that reflects the highest standards of law enforcement.

Excellence

COMMUNITY CONNECTION

Building stronger relationships beyond the badge

The Mangum Police Department is committed to investing in the community through leadership, outreach, education, and programs that bring citizens and law enforcement closer together.

Featured Program

Project S.T.R.O.N.G.

Founded and led by Major Josh Olson, Project S.T.R.O.N.G. is a summer leadership program that teaches young people what service, character, and community leadership look like in action.

Through community service projects, mentoring, speakers, and training opportunities, the program challenges youth to become role models and make a positive difference in Mangum.

Citizens Police Academy

Coming this fall, the first Citizens Police Academy will help residents gain a deeper understanding of policing procedures, laws, and day-to-day department operations.

May Day Madness

Beginning May 1, officers and local kids will meet at the high school parking lot, then head out across town to surprise residents with flowers and a little harmless fun.

Community Engagement

From public events to outreach opportunities, the department is focused on creating stronger relationships with the people of Mangum through visible, positive interaction.
